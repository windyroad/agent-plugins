RFCs surface for ratification on their own — no more remembering.

Captured RFCs are born unconfirmed and are ratified at the `manage-rfc accepted` transition, but nothing reminded you they were waiting. A new SessionStart nudge now counts RFCs lacking human oversight and surfaces them each session — the same self-surfacing pattern already used for unconfirmed ADRs and jobs-to-be-done. It is silent once everything is ratified, and self-suppresses under AFK orchestration like its siblings.

This is the first piece of making the RFC framework self-maintaining (P378 / RFC-030); the derived `## Commits` view and auto-transition follow.
